About me

Hi! I'm Dianwei Chen, a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Prof. Terry Yang. Before joining UMD, I acquired my Master's degree at The Ohio State University, working with Prof. Keith Redmill and Prof. Umit Ozguner, and my Bachelor's degree at Zhejiang University.

My research lies at the intersection of Reinforcement Learning, Digital Twins, Autonomous Vehicle (AV) Safety, and Vision Large Language Models.

I work on building intelligent and robust simulation platforms to evaluate AV safety in complex and adverse driving scenarios. My recent projects include RL-based car-following algorithms in CARLA-SUMO co-simulation environments, Physics Regularized Gaussian Process (PRGP) models, and pedestrian behavior modeling for identifying edge-case AV failures.

Previously, I have also contributed to real-vehicle ROS and Autoware system development, and I am actively involved in bridging the gap between simulation-based research and real-world AV deployment.

Beyond research, I am passionate about building open-source tools for the AV community and advancing safe and trustworthy AI-driven transportation systems.
